There is an operation known as transposition that changes row vectors into column vectors and vice versa. The transpose of a matrix is indicated by a superscript T, the rows of the transposed matrix are the columns of the original matrix and its columns are the original matrix's rows. This idea (interchanging rows and columns) is surprisingly important and we'll be using it quite a bit in the future.
